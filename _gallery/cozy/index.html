It's something...

cozy.nyc is a ongoing project me, Juan Jimenez, and Sophia Setsu are working on mainly to build our web development skills. The site oranigaally was to be an econmerce site to buy and sell clothing, but has changed over time to be more of a, for a lack of a better term, social media site for creatives, makers, and fans alike to discuss and share their and others works.

Currently the features we working on are a messaging board "Boards" and a video streaming directory "Streams". "Works", which is a where users can post they're ongoing and/or finished projects.

Stay Cozy...

Why the name cozy... Well when we first started on the project (when we planned it to be a ecommerce site) Cozy Tapes by A$AP MOB was out during the time.

Dosis

Why Dosis... Dosis is a rounded san-serif font. When thinking about creating the site to invoke a warm and playful atmosphere. Dosis extra bold fits well with the astitcs and the rounded nature of the logo, the cozy cube.

Cubes

Designed by Byron Kim

Why cubes... When thinking about the design of the cube, we wanted to have a logo that could be converted to a physical object. A cube is a verstial and simple shape to produce on many mediums.

Having the one side with the letter C is a play on the idea that cozy content is consumed on a digital box of some sort like a computer monitor or a phone screen.

Boards

An image board where users can create threads to discuss topics on the site, wither it be music, science, politcs, or random shenanigans. The goal behind the design/layout is to recreate a bulletin board

Similar to chan and forum sites, boards has a list of catagories to create a thread on. Threads in a catagory/board are ordered by recent activity. Post in the thread are ordered by creation.

Works

Still in the works

Works for or makers, creatives, researchers, and everyone inbetween to share they're projects that are in the works or complete.

Content nagivation is visualed on a map of a city, with an addition list feed. Users can create "Blocks" which take a empty lot on the map. The block contains the users project which could be audio, video, photo, or text/pdf with an addition to a blurb, users who worked on the project, and links to external sites. There's also a comment section for users to provide feedback on projects.
