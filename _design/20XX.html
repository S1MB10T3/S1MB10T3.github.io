20XX magazine is a publication showcasing New York City culture, artists, and events with a focus on native New Yorkers of color. 20XX magazine have been posting articles on Medium and released their first print issue on Oct. 23rd 2020.

The Start

Minimal. Dynamic. Playful.

When discussing the goal of the site, we came to the conclusion that the site should represent the future inspired by video games like Mega Man Battle Network, Jet Set Radio and NieR:Automata. Aiming for a clean and simple style that is also playful with animations and color choices.

WWhen starting the design process I asked Mathew, the magazine founder to put together a mood board before our initial design meeting to get an idea of the visual/UI aspects of the site.

During the initial meeting we came to the conclusion that the site should focus on being more aesthetic/UI focus. The idea of a Navi "AI" similar to "Clippy" from Microsoft Office to help guide the user around.

The Process

After discussing the his goals for his publication, I built out a general sitemap for someone visting the site along with wireframes. We also discussed other elements of the site such as typefaces to use and what websites inspire us for this project.

The Results

From Home to Featured

The featured article will be a simple scroll/drag from the homepage (which displays the title and images from that article) with all other articles being accessible from the article directory via Navi.

Navigational Assistant - Navi

"2wen-t" Modeled by Jonathan Yuen

To make the site playful and Dynamic we discussed adding a navigational assistant instead of a simple navbar or menu. The 20XX Team designed 2wen-t. I modified the textures to fit the color theme, the menu face for when it's not active, and the navigational/UX interactions.

Article Directory

TThe Article Directory displays all articles published on the site with 2wen-t gaining a search feature to query content.

Gallery

The gallery section of the site is for image heavy collections. With art and photography in the center of content.

Post Mortem

While I left this as a note for the developer, 2wen-t needs to be more noticable. One way to do so is to have dialog boxes pop up when the user first visits the site. Something along the lines of "Hi, click me!"... idk something cute.

Another thing is that I wish I provided research for 20XX Mag because a month later someone on the team had mentioned that the magazine with that name has already existed. While the name was chosen and printing of the magazine started long before me, I felt like it should be my goal to guide all my future clients on branding even though I wasn't specifically hired to do so.
