New York Film & Music Foundation is a nonprofit accelerator geared toward helping artists find success. NYFMF goal is to help artists tell their stories to a broader audience, and connect them to a community that appreciates the arts.

The Start

Understanding the Brand

When I brought on to work at New York Film Foundation's I hosted a workshop on basic design, design thinking, market research, and brand identity. The goal was to make sure that the team has a good base for when I leave to take over and run the site. Also due to a lack of any design language or style guide, graphics and messaging on social media was all over the place.

During the Brand Identity portion of the workshop I went over the what is and makes up a brand. With the team, we came up with a handful of brand attributes to help guide me and the rest of the organization.

Research

Two weeks before the workshop I asked the team to provide websites that they like the visual and functional aesthetics of from the nonprofit and music/media sectors. During the kickoff meeting I presented a few of these websites and a couple of websites of my own choosing and ran an exercise on what aspects of the website did they like and dislike.

Previous Brand

Along with all the research for building a new site, I also conducted interviews with the team members and friends on the current brand and in particular the logo.

While I could understand where the original designer was going with a FM logo, the way it was executed makes it look more like a FN or a FA to mulputlie people both within the team and non designer friends. I do like the choice of helvetica, since it's the typeface found on every corner and tunnel in NYC.

Previous Site

While the aesthetics of the site can be argued for, the functionality of it for the team is lacking. The site was built with Express.js and Jade, while easy for a coder to manage, quite difficult for someone on the social media team to update info on the site or change the roster of artists. Another issue is the one page layout.

While mainly organizations can get away if a one page site, NYFMF and many nonprofits have to showcase more information to showcase value then what can fit on a one page layout.

The Process

Wireframes

This time around, I tried out a new process in the design creation. After creating the wireframes, I went straight into creating a design/brand identity for NYFMF.

Something I won't repeat again is creating a brand identity in one shot without drafts for the client to choose between. But due to a lack of time I decided to go with a single identity draft to present.

The Results

Brand Redesign

Logo

With the goal of using the brand attributes as presented earlier in the case study, the new Logo hopefully instills a "Modern" and "Establish" feeling with the type choice ofLemon Milk by Marsnev.

The goal of the new logo mark wasn't to be extremely iconic, but more serves a functional purpose to fit different needs in film and graphics. By default it uses different shades of grey to keep it dynamatic.

A concept of how the logo can be used in graphics such as posters or promotional material! Inspired by the MTV logo, a system where a logo can be manipulated around and still hold on its own is something I try to build towards.

Color

When discussing colors with the team, I came to the conclusion that keeping black and white allows for the images and film to breathe more without worry of clash.

Typography

Lemon Milk - Logo

Neue Haas Unica W1G - Headings

Cooper Hewitt - Body

This time around I tried to keep things simple with the type. Focusing on just two fonts choices, three if you count the logo.

I have conquered using a serif font for the body but will be looking for a professional. I think using a san-serif like Cooper Hewitt suits the branding a bit more since the organization is pretty new and isn't trying to be "elities" or "high end".

Website Redesign

Homepage displays the most recent Deep Dive Sessions on the top via a carousel, which are "blog post"/articles about an Artist involved in NYFMF. The homepage design accomplishes the same needed info about the organization in a cleaner format while also leading towards other pages on the site for more details.

In the original wireframes, I had a place for artist content. Instead I opted for having links to external sites for artist content. Since the organization isn't a publishing company and all the artists that are supported are creating and publishing on their own, it would be easy for development and for the artists to have users of the site go to their Youtube channels or Soundcloud profiles. This way we wouldn't need to build a publishing platform on the site.

An actual donation page. In terms of the rest of the donation process, i.e. name and email, that's to be planned once the choice of donation platform is solidified.
