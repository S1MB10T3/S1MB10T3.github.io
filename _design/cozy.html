It's something...

cozy.nyc is a ongoing project Juan Jimenez, Sophia Setsu, and I are working on. The site was originally intended to be an online buying and selling marketplace to a platform for creatives, makers, and fans alike to discuss and share creative projects. Now it's just a livestream platform focused for creatives in NYC.

You can read more about the road of failures on cozy on my blog here.

New Goal

Our new goal with cozy.nyc is to build a open livestreaming platform for NYC creatives to show finished work or the creative procress.

Free and Open Source

cozy.nyc is under the GPL license, thus feel free to use our project files for your own needs and/or help us out..

Brand Design


Stay Cozy...

Why the name "cozy?" When we first started on the project (back when we planned it to be am e-commerce site), A$AP Mob's *Cozy Tapes* just came out.

Logo - Cubes

Designed by Byron Kim

Why a cube? We wanted to have a logo that could be converted to a physical object, and a cube is a versatile and simple shape to reproduce using other media.

The "C" on the side of the cube is a play on the fact that cozy's content is consumed on digital boxes like computer monitors and phones.

Font - Dosis

Why Dosis? Dosis is a rounded, san-serif font. When considering how to give the site a warm, playful atmosphere, Dosis Extra Bold fits well and matches other design elements like our logo.

The Old Web Design

Users can post livestreams from websites like Twitch, Youtube, and Instagram to the directory and invite other users to the streaming platform's chat.

The goal is to have a community that can watch and discuss videos and streams together as they're happening, without having to move around different websites.

Wireframes

Old Design Mockups

One issue with the original cozy.nyc designs was that I attemped to combine to many colors. While every section of the site, i.e. streams, boards, etc, had their own color theme, the themes themselves did not work so much with the content diplayed and the goals of the application.

Since the team is focused on just the livestreaming features of the site the old design did not have to hold to the same color theme rules.

Redesign

Home Page

The homepage/stream directory is now focued on displaying all current streams at the ease of a scroll. A recent caputure of the stream is displayed and info such as the name, user, and views of the view is hidden until hovered. This hopefully will put the content front and center rather then the views and whos hosting the stream.

The order and size of the stream preview is based on views for now until a better solution of order to get more people on to unheard artist is found.

Stream

A global chat is still kept for a sense of community amongst the site. There's also a local/profile chat if a user allows it on their stream along with support for 3rd Party chats from other platforms the video stream is taking place.

A simple profile to get users on to other platforms to support artist. The goal is not trying to be the next big platform for artist. It's just a place to share content across a community big or small.
