The Process

Moodboard

During the first design meeting with Salesforce, I created a moodboard to go over possible character styles for both the players and Non-Playable Characters, video games, older Forio situations, and some rough mockups to showcase case possible design solution for activities and features for each activity.

With the 1st draft of the simulation architecture, I created a User Flow to plan out the path and gameplay for the players. Sadly I no longer have access to the a copy of the User Flow used for this project.

Wireframes

Low fidelity Wireframes to flesh out the UX of the simulation. This early start was give the clients a rough insight to the the gameplay of the game as well as allow a space for open feedback and gameplay decision.

Higher fidelity Wire Frames to move the designs closer to the final product and a start to aligning the UI to Salesforce’s Lighting Design.

The Results

Style

Sticking to salesforce branding color scheme.

Layout

Cards

To keep it inline with Salesforce designs for their platform/Lighting Design. Easy animation for the developers to implate while add more dymatic flair to the expirence

Activities

Gameplay decisions are made in the Decisions Page. This changes based on the activity.

Activity 1 Decision was for players to pick challenges that the fictional company Allin faces. Players can drag and drop the challenge cards in order from 1-4.

Activity 3 has players interviewing Allin Employee NPCs with simulated responses. First by picking a challenge then a question. A NPC can ether respond with helpful insight to the question, point the player in to the right person to ask, or have a non-response.

Metrics

Teams are able to keep track of they KPIs to see how well they doing and get feedback on prior decisions made on the KPI Page. They’re also able to check how much total knowledge they’ve unlocked through the game through the Knowledge Page.
